## Art-Projects

## Mirror

🎨🪞 This project was created during my first year at university and involved choosing an object from a story we were given, then designing concept art based around it. I decided to focus on the mirror because I loved the idea of combining a natural, earthy aesthetic with an object that reflects beauty and emotion. 🌿✨

To create the final concept piece, I first built a simple 3D model of the mirror so I could capture all the angles and perspectives I needed. This allowed me to produce a more complete and detailed concept design that showcased every part of the object. 🛠️📐

Using a 3D base as part of my concept art workflow became a technique I relied on heavily throughout university, and it’s still something I use to this day. Not only does it speed up the design process, but it also creates a much stronger foundation to work from when developing ideas and refining details. 🚀🎨 By combining 3D modelling with digital painting, I’m able to create designs that feel more structured, believable, and visually polished.

## Make Art Not War

🎨✨ This piece of art was created during college for a competition, and it also marked my very first digital painting. It was the project that truly made me fall in love with digital art and inspired me to continue developing my creative skills in this medium. 🖌️💻

The artwork explores the contrast between good and evil, while also highlighting the connection that can exist between opposites. Rather than focusing purely on conflict, the piece represents the idea that understanding and unity can bridge even the strongest divides. ⚖️🌌
